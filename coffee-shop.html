Overview

Project Background

This project analyzes six months of transactional data from a coffee shop chain operating three branches across New York City — Hell's Kitchen, Lower Manhattan, and Astoria. The goal was to transform raw sales records into a fully interactive Power BI report that allows stakeholders to explore performance by time, location, product category, and order detail. The result is a two-dashboard business intelligence solution that reveals where revenue is generated, which products drive the most volume, and how sales fluctuate across the calendar month.

Key Findings

What the Data Revealed

Six months of data uncovered clear behavioral and revenue patterns that directly inform inventory, staffing, and marketing decisions.

☕
Coffee Dominates Revenue

Coffee alone accounts for $270K (39%) of total revenue, followed by Tea at $200K. These two categories combined drive nearly 70% of all income — making them the core strategic focus.

📍
Even Location Distribution

All three branches perform remarkably evenly — Hell's Kitchen leads at 34%, followed by Astoria (33.9%) and Lower Manhattan (32.9%) — indicating consistent brand performance across neighborhoods.

📈
Strong Revenue Growth

Monthly sales grew steadily from $82K in January to a peak of $166K in June — doubling over six months, suggesting strong seasonal momentum heading into summer.

🕐
Morning Rush Peak

Order data reveals the highest transaction volumes between 9–11 AM, with top single orders exceeding $1,400. This peak window is critical for staffing and inventory readiness.

📦
Long-Tail Product Mix

Beyond core drinks, Bakery contributes $80K and Drinking Chocolate $70K. Niche items like Coffee Beans and Flavours each generate under $10K — signaling potential for portfolio optimization.

🔄
Order Consistency

Daily order counts hold steady between 4K–5K orders per day through most of the period, with a notable dip in late June — likely reflecting end-of-period data or operational shifts.

Methodology

How It Was Built

A structured data pipeline from raw transactional records to polished interactive dashboards.

01
Data Collection & Exploration

Sourced six months of raw transactional records containing order timestamps, product categories, quantities, prices, and branch locations. Initial exploration identified data quality issues including duplicates, null values, and inconsistent category naming.

02
Data Cleaning with Power Query

Used Power Query to standardize column types, resolve naming inconsistencies across product categories, remove duplicate records, and build a clean date dimension table to enable proper time intelligence calculations.

03
Data Modeling & DAX

Established a star schema data model connecting fact and dimension tables. Wrote DAX measures for total revenue, average order value, monthly growth rate, and location share — enabling flexible, performant filtering across all visuals.

04
Dashboard Design & SQL Validation

Designed two thematic dashboards in Power BI — a Sales Dashboard focusing on revenue and category trends, and an Orders Dashboard covering volume and location distribution. SQL queries were used to cross-validate key aggregates before finalizing visuals.

05
Interactivity & Storytelling

Added slicers for Category, Type, and Detail filters alongside month-based navigation buttons, allowing users to slice the data dynamically. Cross-filtering between charts creates a self-contained analytical story within each dashboard page.
